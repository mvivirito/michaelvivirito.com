About Me

I'm a Lead Site Reliability Engineer with a decade of professional IT experience. I've been at a keyboard since I was three years old, and I've spent most of my adult life trying to understand how the layers underneath a running service actually work — from packet to process to platform.

Most of my hands-on work today sits at two ends of the same spectrum: Kubernetes and AWS on the production side, where I run highly available platforms for a living, and FreeBSD and networking on the homelab side, where I run a self-built pf router and a fleet of small services that exist mostly to keep me sharp.

I'm mostly self-taught but I started formal CS coursework in 2017 and finished a Bachelor's in Computer Science along with Associate degrees in CS, Math, and Physics. I'm AWS-certified, a long-time FreeBSD advocate, and a committed Vim golfer.

When I'm not on call I'm usually iterating on the network — building out the FreeBSD pf router, writing about the pf rules that hold it together, or refining the NixOS configs that define everything behind it.

Homelab — FreeBSD pf Router

At the edge of my homelab is a fanless FreeBSD box that handles every packet coming into and out of the network — WAN router, stateful pf firewall, recursive DNS, DHCP, NTP, and a WireGuard endpoint. No vendor GUI, no shadow configs. Just /etc/rc.conf, /etc/pf.conf, and a git repo. 

            ┌─────────────────────┐
            │      Internet       │
            └──────────┬──────────┘
                       │ igc0 (WAN)
            ┌──────────┴──────────┐
            │   FreeBSD Router    │
            │   pf · unbound      │
            │   dhcpd · ntpd      │
            └──┬──────┬──────┬────┘
        igc1   │   igc2  │   igc3
       (LAN)   │ (servers)   (IoT)

Four physical interfaces, four broadcast domains, default-deny everywhere. IoT traffic is rule-prevented from talking to anything but the internet. Boot environments via ZFS make every change reversible with a reboot.
